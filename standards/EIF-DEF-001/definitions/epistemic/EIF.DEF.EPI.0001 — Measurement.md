# EIF.DEF.EPI.0001 — Measurement

## Canonical Definition

> **Measurement** is the disciplined engineering activity that acquires evidence from Engineering Reality by determining the value, condition, property, or characteristic of one or more aspects of Engineering Reality at one or more Temporal Extents.

---

## Intent

Measurement establishes the entry point of Engineering Perception.

It provides the disciplined means through which Engineering Intelligence begins acquiring evidence from Engineering Reality.

Measurement does not interpret, understand, or explain Engineering Reality.

Its sole purpose is to acquire evidence faithfully from what exists.

---

## Governing Responsibility

Measurement uniquely **acquires** evidence from Engineering Reality.

It neither manifests observations nor derives understanding.

Its sole responsibility is to acquire evidence that faithfully reflects Engineering Reality.

---

## Characteristics

Measurement:

- acquires evidence from Engineering Reality;
- operates upon one or more Engineering States or Engineering Events;
- occurs at one or more Temporal Extents;
- may employ physical, digital, computational, observational, analytical, or human measurement mechanisms;
- precedes Observation within Engineering Perception;
- does not itself interpret the acquired evidence.

## Temporal Perspective

Measurement occurs at one or more **Temporal Extents**.

The acquired evidence reflects Engineering Reality only for the Temporal Extent during which the measurement was performed.

Different Temporal Extents may therefore produce different measurements of the same Engineering Reality.

---

## Examples

### Software Engineering

Collecting CPU utilization, memory consumption, deployment duration, defect counts, code coverage, or build execution time constitutes Measurement.

---

### Manufacturing Engineering

Measuring machine vibration, dimensional tolerance, production throughput, equipment temperature, or product weight constitutes Measurement.

---

### Enterprise Architecture

Measuring application availability, process execution time, portfolio cost, service response time, or business capability maturity constitutes Measurement.

---

## Non-examples

### Observation

Observation manifests evidence acquired through Measurement.

It is not the activity of acquiring that evidence.

---

### Engineering Understanding

Engineering Understanding contextualizes Knowledge.

It does not acquire evidence from Engineering Reality.

---

### Reasoning

Reasoning derives conclusions from Engineering Understanding.

It is not Measurement.

---

## Notes

### Note 1 — Measurement Precedes Observation

Measurement acquires evidence.

Observation manifests that acquired evidence.

The two concepts are distinct and shall not be treated as interchangeable.

---

### Note 2 — Independence of Reality

Measurement acquires evidence from Engineering Reality but does not influence the existence of Engineering Reality itself.

Reality exists independently of whether it has been measured.

---

### Note 3 — Temporal Validity

Every Measurement is valid only for the Temporal Extent during which it was performed.

Consequently, repeated Measurements across different Temporal Extents may acquire different evidence while referring to the same Engineering Reality.
