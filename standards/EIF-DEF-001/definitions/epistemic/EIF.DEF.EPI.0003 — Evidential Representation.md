# EIF.DEF.EPI.0003 — Evidential Representation

## Canonical Definition

> **Evidential Representation** is the preserved representation of observable evidence manifested through Observation, enabling Engineering Intelligence to subsequently discover Knowledge, Understanding, Reasoning, and Insight from Engineering Reality.

---

## Intent

Evidential Representation establishes the final stage of Engineering Perception.

It preserves observable evidence as a durable engineering representation that can subsequently participate in Engineering Discovery.

Evidential Representation neither interprets nor explains Engineering Reality.

Its sole purpose is to preserve observable evidence faithfully for subsequent Engineering Discovery.

---

## Governing Responsibility

Evidential Representation uniquely **preserves** observable evidence manifested through Observation.

It neither acquires evidence nor derives Knowledge.

Its sole responsibility is to preserve observable evidence in a form suitable for Engineering Discovery.

---

## Characteristics

Evidential Representation:

- preserves observable evidence;
- originates from one or more Observations;
- represents one or more aspects of Engineering Reality;
- exists independently of whether Engineering Discovery has yet occurred;
- may be physical, digital, graphical, textual, numerical, symbolic, or hybrid;
- exists throughout one or more Temporal Extents.

## Temporal Perspective

Evidential Representations exist throughout one or more **Temporal Extents**.

They preserve observable evidence acquired at specific Temporal Extents while remaining available for future Engineering Discovery.

---

## Examples

### Software Engineering

Execution logs, telemetry streams, source code snapshots, architecture diagrams, build reports, defect records, deployment histories, and performance dashboards each constitute Evidential Representations.

---

### Manufacturing Engineering

Sensor histories, inspection reports, machine logs, production records, calibration certificates, and quality control charts each constitute Evidential Representations.

---

### Enterprise Architecture

Capability maps, application inventories, process models, architecture diagrams, governance records, dependency maps, and portfolio dashboards each constitute Evidential Representations.

---

## Non-examples

### Observation

Observation manifests observable evidence.

It does not preserve that evidence.

---

### Knowledge

Knowledge synthesizes one or more Evidential Representations.

Knowledge is therefore distinct from the preserved evidence itself.

---

### Engineering Understanding

Engineering Understanding contextualizes Knowledge.

It is not an Evidential Representation.

---

## Notes

### Note 1 — Representation versus Interpretation

Evidential Representation preserves observable evidence.

Interpretation belongs exclusively to Engineering Discovery.

---

### Note 2 — Durability

Engineering Perception may be transient.

Evidential Representation provides the durable engineering memory from which Engineering Discovery subsequently operates.

---

### Note 3 — Independence of Discovery

Evidential Representations may exist without ever being interpreted.

Their existence does not imply that Knowledge or Insight has yet been discovered.
