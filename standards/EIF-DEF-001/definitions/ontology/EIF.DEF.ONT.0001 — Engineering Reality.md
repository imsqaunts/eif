# EIF.DEF.ONT.0001 — Engineering Reality

## Canonical Definition

> **Engineering Reality** is the totality of all engineering existence within a defined scope and Temporal Extent, comprising Engineering Systems, Engineering Objects, Engineering Relationships, Engineering Boundaries, Engineering Environments, Engineering States, and Engineering Events, independent of whether they have been observed, measured, understood, or acted upon.

---

## Intent

Engineering Reality establishes the ontological foundation of the Engineering Intelligence Framework (EIF).

It defines the complete domain of engineering existence upon which Engineering Perception, Engineering Discovery, Engineering Operations, and Engineering Evolution operate.

Engineering Reality exists independently of Engineering Intelligence and therefore constitutes the objective basis from which all engineering understanding and action emerge.

---

## Governing Responsibility

Engineering Reality uniquely **exists** as the complete domain of engineering existence.

It neither performs Engineering Perception, nor participates in Engineering Discovery, nor executes Engineering Operations.

Its sole responsibility is to provide the objective ontological foundation from which Engineering Intelligence acquires understanding and upon which Engineering Intelligence acts.

---

## Characteristics

Engineering Reality:

- exists independently of Engineering Intelligence;
- exists within one or more Temporal Extents;
- comprises Engineering Systems, Engineering Objects, Engineering Relationships, Engineering Boundaries, Engineering Environments, Engineering States, and Engineering Events;
- may evolve over time through Engineering Events and Engineering Evolution;
- may be partially or completely unknown to Engineering Intelligence;
- remains unaffected by whether it has been perceived, measured, represented, or understood.

## Temporal Perspective

Engineering Reality exists throughout one or more **Temporal Extents**.

Its existence is independent of observation and persists regardless of whether Engineering Intelligence has perceived or understood it.

Engineering Reality may change through Engineering Events and may undergo sustained transformation through Engineering Evolution.

---

## Examples

### Software Engineering

A software delivery organization, including its applications, repositories, infrastructure, development processes, dependencies, and operational constraints, constitutes Engineering Reality regardless of whether every aspect has been measured or understood.

---

### Manufacturing Engineering

A production facility, including machines, tooling, materials, workflows, environmental conditions, and production constraints, exists as Engineering Reality even before engineering measurements are performed.

---

### Enterprise Architecture

An enterprise consisting of business capabilities, information systems, organizational structures, integrations, policies, and stakeholders forms Engineering Reality independent of any architectural model describing it.

---

## Non-examples

### Engineering Understanding

Engineering Understanding is a cognitive interpretation of Engineering Reality.

It is not Engineering Reality itself.

---

### Engineering Model

A model is a representation of selected aspects of Engineering Reality.

It is not Engineering Reality itself.

---

### Engineering Documentation

Engineering documentation describes Engineering Reality.

It does not constitute Engineering Reality.

---

## Notes

### Note 1 — Independence of Existence

Engineering Reality exists independently of Engineering Perception, Engineering Discovery, Engineering Operations, or Engineering Evolution.

Its existence does not depend upon human or artificial awareness.

---

### Note 2 — Scope

Engineering Reality is always considered within a defined engineering scope.

Different scopes may define different Engineering Realities while remaining consistent with the same ontological principles.

---

### Note 3 — Foundational Concept

Engineering Reality is the foundational concept of the Engineering Intelligence Framework.

Every canonical concept defined within EIF ultimately derives from, operates upon, or contributes to Engineering Reality.
