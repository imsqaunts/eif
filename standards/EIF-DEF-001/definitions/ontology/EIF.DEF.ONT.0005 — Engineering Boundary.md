# EIF.DEF.ONT.0005 — Engineering Boundary

## Canonical Definition

> **Engineering Boundary** is the ontological delimiter that defines the scope, extent, and identity of Engineering Reality, Engineering Systems, Engineering Objects, or Engineering Relationships by distinguishing what is included from what is excluded across one or more Temporal Extents.

---

## Intent

Engineering Boundary establishes the limits within which Engineering Reality is defined and reasoned about.

It provides the ontological mechanism by which engineering scope is determined, enabling Engineering Intelligence to distinguish the subject of Engineering Discovery from its surrounding context.

Engineering Boundaries ensure that Engineering Reality is always considered relative to an explicitly defined scope.

---

## Governing Responsibility

Engineering Boundary uniquely **delimits** Engineering Reality and its constituent concepts.

It neither organizes Engineering Systems nor connects Engineering Objects.

Its sole responsibility is to establish the ontological limits that distinguish engineering existence from its surrounding context.

---

## Characteristics

Engineering Boundaries:

- delimit Engineering Reality, Engineering Systems, Engineering Objects, and Engineering Relationships;
- distinguish what is included from what is excluded;
- establish engineering scope;
- may represent physical, logical, organizational, informational, contractual, regulatory, or conceptual limits;
- may evolve through Engineering Events;
- exist throughout one or more Temporal Extents.

## Temporal Perspective

Engineering Boundaries exist throughout one or more **Temporal Extents**.

Boundaries may expand, contract, merge, divide, or otherwise evolve through Engineering Events while continuing to define the scope of Engineering Reality.

---

## Examples

### Software Engineering

The boundary of a software platform may include its applications, repositories, infrastructure, deployment pipelines, and governance while excluding external vendor systems.

---

### Manufacturing Engineering

A production line boundary may include machines, operators, materials, tooling, and quality stations while excluding upstream suppliers and downstream logistics.

---

### Enterprise Architecture

A business capability boundary may encompass business processes, applications, organizational units, and information assets while excluding capabilities owned by external organizations.

---

## Non-examples

### Engineering Environment

The Engineering Environment provides context surrounding Engineering Reality.

It does not define the scope of Engineering Reality itself.

---

### Engineering Relationship

Engineering Relationships connect Engineering Objects.

They do not delimit engineering scope.

---

### Engineering State

Engineering State represents the condition of Engineering Reality.

It does not establish ontological limits.

---

## Notes

### Note 1 — Scope Precedes Discovery

Engineering Discovery always operates within explicitly or implicitly defined Engineering Boundaries.

Without a Boundary, Engineering Reality cannot be unambiguously identified.

---

### Note 2 — Multiple Boundaries

Engineering Reality may simultaneously exist within multiple Engineering Boundaries representing different engineering perspectives, organizational responsibilities, regulatory jurisdictions, or architectural viewpoints.

---

### Note 3 — Context Separation

Engineering Boundaries distinguish Engineering Reality from its surrounding Engineering Environment.

The Environment provides context; the Boundary determines scope.
