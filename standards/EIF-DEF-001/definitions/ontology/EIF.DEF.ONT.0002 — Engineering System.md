# EIF.DEF.ONT.0002 — Engineering System

## Canonical Definition

> **Engineering System** is an organized composition of one or more Engineering Objects, Engineering Relationships, Engineering Boundaries, and Engineering Environments that collectively exist and interact within Engineering Reality to realize one or more engineering purposes across one or more Temporal Extents.

---

## Intent

Engineering System establishes the primary organizational construct of Engineering Reality.

It provides the structural context within which Engineering Objects, Relationships, Boundaries, States, and Events coexist and interact to realize engineering behaviour and purpose.

Engineering Systems enable Engineering Intelligence to reason about engineering existence as coherent wholes rather than isolated entities.

---

## Governing Responsibility

Engineering System uniquely **organizes** Engineering Reality into coherent engineering structures.

It neither defines individual Engineering Objects nor performs Engineering Operations.

Its sole responsibility is to organize engineering existence into meaningful compositions capable of exhibiting engineering behaviour.

---

## Characteristics

Engineering Systems:

- organize one or more Engineering Objects;
- establish one or more Engineering Relationships;
- are delimited by one or more Engineering Boundaries;
- exist within one or more Engineering Environments;
- possess one or more Engineering States;
- experience one or more Engineering Events;
- may contain one or more Engineering Subsystems;
- may themselves participate as Engineering Objects within larger Engineering Systems;
- exist across one or more Temporal Extents.

## Temporal Perspective

Engineering Systems exist across one or more **Temporal Extents**.

Their organization may evolve through Engineering Events and Engineering Evolution while preserving their engineering identity or giving rise to new Engineering Systems.

---

## Examples

### Software Engineering

A software platform consisting of applications, services, repositories, deployment pipelines, infrastructure, and operational policies constitutes an Engineering System.

---

### Manufacturing Engineering

An automated production line comprising machines, robots, sensors, conveyors, quality stations, and operators forms an Engineering System.

---

### Enterprise Architecture

An enterprise capability consisting of business processes, information systems, organizational units, governance structures, and supporting technologies constitutes an Engineering System.

---

## Non-examples

### Engineering Object

An Engineering Object is an individual constituent of an Engineering System.

It is not the organized composition itself.

---

### Engineering Relationship

An Engineering Relationship connects Engineering Objects.

It does not organize them into an Engineering System.

---

### Engineering Boundary

An Engineering Boundary delimits an Engineering System.

It is not the Engineering System itself.

---

## Notes

### Note 1 — Organizational Identity

Engineering Systems derive their identity from the organization of their constituent Engineering Objects and Relationships rather than from any individual constituent alone.

---

### Note 2 — Hierarchical Composition

Engineering Systems may be recursively composed.

An Engineering System may contain Engineering Subsystems while simultaneously participating as an Engineering Object within a larger Engineering System.

---

### Note 3 — Purpose

Engineering Systems organize engineering existence to realize one or more engineering purposes.

Purpose provides context for organization but does not define the ontological existence of the Engineering System.
