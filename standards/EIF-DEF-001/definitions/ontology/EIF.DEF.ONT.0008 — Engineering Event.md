# EIF.DEF.ONT.0008 — Engineering Event

## Canonical Definition

> **Engineering Event** is the ontological manifestation through which change occurs within Engineering Reality by changing the Engineering State of Engineering Reality, an Engineering System, or an Engineering Object at one or more Temporal Extents.

---

## Intent

Engineering Event establishes the ontological mechanism through which Engineering Reality changes.

It provides the basis for understanding how Engineering States transition over time while remaining independent of whether those changes arise intentionally or unintentionally, internally or externally.

Engineering Events therefore constitute the dynamic foundation upon which Engineering Perception perceives change, Engineering Discovery discovers change, and Engineering Operations realize intentional transformation.

---

## Governing Responsibility

Engineering Event uniquely **changes** Engineering States within Engineering Reality.

It neither represents Engineering States nor captures operational consequences.

Its sole responsibility is to manifest the ontological change through which Engineering Reality transitions between Engineering States.

---

## Characteristics

Engineering Events:

- change one or more Engineering States;
- occur within Engineering Reality;
- may affect one or more Engineering Systems or Engineering Objects;
- may arise intentionally or unintentionally;
- may originate internally or externally to an Engineering Boundary;
- exist independently of whether they have been perceived, represented, or understood;
- occur throughout one or more Temporal Extents.

## Temporal Perspective

Engineering Events occur throughout one or more **Temporal Extents**.

Each Engineering Event represents the ontological transition between Engineering States and therefore provides the mechanism through which Engineering Reality changes over time.

---

## Examples

### Software Engineering

A production deployment, service outage, security breach, database migration, configuration change, or infrastructure failure each constitutes an Engineering Event because it changes the Engineering State of the software platform.

---

### Manufacturing Engineering

A machine failure, tooling replacement, production start, material arrival, equipment calibration, or quality deviation each constitutes an Engineering Event.

---

### Enterprise Architecture

An organizational restructuring, application deployment, policy change, business acquisition, or capability retirement each constitutes an Engineering Event.

---

## Non-examples

### Engineering State

Engineering State represents the condition of Engineering Reality.

It does not change that condition.

---

### Engineering Action

Engineering Action intentionally executes an operational intervention.

It may result in one or more Engineering Events but is not itself an Engineering Event.

---

### Engineering Outcome

Engineering Outcome captures the realized consequence of one or more Engineering Actions.

It is not the ontological manifestation through which change occurs.

---

## Notes

### Note 1 — Event versus Action

Engineering Actions are intentional operational activities.

Engineering Events are ontological manifestations through which change occurs.

An Engineering Action may result in one or more Engineering Events, but the two concepts remain semantically distinct.

---

### Note 2 — Independence of Occurrence

Engineering Events occur regardless of whether they are perceived, represented, or understood.

Engineering Perception and Engineering Discovery operate upon Engineering Reality after Engineering Events have occurred; they do not cause Engineering Events.

---

### Note 3 — Change versus Transformation

Engineering Events change Engineering States.

Engineering Evolution establishes the sustained transformation of Engineering Reality resulting from realized Engineering Outcomes.

Change and transformation therefore represent distinct ontological and operational concepts within EIF.
