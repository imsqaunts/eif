# EIF.DEF.OPR.0001 — Engineering Decision

## Canonical Definition

> **Engineering Decision** is the intentional operational commitment that emerges from one or more Engineering Insights, establishing the chosen course of action through which Engineering Intelligence intends to transform Engineering Reality.

---

## Intent

Engineering Decision establishes the entry point of Engineering Operations.

It converts one or more Engineering Insights into an intentional operational commitment that precedes Engineering Action.

Engineering Decision does not itself transform Engineering Reality.

Its sole purpose is to establish an intentional commitment to act.

---

## Governing Responsibility

Engineering Decision uniquely **commits** Engineering Intelligence to an intentional course of action.

It neither derives Engineering Insight nor executes Engineering Action.

Its sole responsibility is to establish the operational commitment through which Engineering Intelligence intends to transform Engineering Reality.

---

## Characteristics

Engineering Decision:

- emerges from one or more Engineering Insights;
- establishes an intentional operational commitment;
- selects one or more courses of Engineering Action;
- may balance opportunities, risks, constraints, trade-offs, and objectives;
- precedes Engineering Action;
- exists throughout one or more Temporal Extents until superseded, fulfilled, or withdrawn.

---

## Relationships

| Relationship | Target |

---

## Temporal Perspective

Engineering Decisions exist throughout one or more **Temporal Extents**.

A Decision remains valid until it is executed, superseded, withdrawn, or otherwise resolved through Engineering Operations.

---

## Examples

### Software Engineering

Approving the migration from a monolithic architecture to microservices after evaluating engineering insights constitutes an Engineering Decision.

---

### Manufacturing Engineering

Approving the redesign of a production line to eliminate identified bottlenecks constitutes an Engineering Decision.

---

### Enterprise Architecture

Approving the consolidation of enterprise applications after architectural assessment constitutes an Engineering Decision.

---

## Non-examples

### Insight

Engineering Insight recognizes engineering significance.

Engineering Decision establishes the intentional commitment to act upon that significance.

---

### Engineering Action

Engineering Action executes an Engineering Decision.

The Decision itself does not transform Engineering Reality.

---

### Engineering Outcome

Engineering Outcome captures the realized consequence of one or more Engineering Actions.

It is not the commitment that precedes those actions.

---

## Notes

### Note 1 — Commitment versus Action

Engineering Decision establishes commitment.

Engineering Action executes that commitment.

The two concepts are therefore operationally distinct.

---

### Note 2 — Emergence from Discovery

Engineering Decisions emerge from Engineering Discovery through one or more Engineering Insights.

Engineering Operations therefore remain grounded in disciplined Engineering Discovery.

---

### Note 3 — Operational Bridge

Engineering Decision establishes the semantic bridge between Engineering Discovery and Engineering Operations.

Without Engineering Decision, Engineering Insight cannot intentionally transform Engineering Reality.
